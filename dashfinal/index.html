All stages are reversible. Unwind in reverse order as signals clear.
1
QUALITY / VALUE TILT
Rotate within equity sleeve. Overweight AVLV and SCHD. Reduce ITOT and QGRW proportionally. No allocation change — equity % stays at model target. Applies all profiles.
2
CASH / SHORT-DURATION INCREASE
Raise cash and/or VTIP by up to +5%, funded from equity. Duration-neutral to duration-reducing. Builds dry powder for redeployment when signals clear. Applies all profiles — Conservative included.
3
BUFFERED ETF TRANCHES
Deploy Innovator 15% buffer / 10–12% cap ETFs in 5–7% increments from ITOT/QGRW (US equity sleeve only). Conservative · Moderate · Mod Growth: up to 20% of portfolio. Growth: up to 10%. Aggressive: not eligible. Sustained stress (>2 quarters) before deploying tranche 2.
4
DD ETFs NOT YET OPERATIONAL
Dual Directional ETFs (Innovator). Growth and Aggressive profiles only. Up to 10% of portfolio. Funded from ITOT. Reserved for confirmed negative regime. Shown in model grid for reference — not yet deployed in practice.

DATA SOURCES
M1
S&P 500 ↗
FRED · SP500 · Auto · Daily · 30-day momentum %
S&P 500 30-day price momentum. Captures the direction of the broad large-cap market. Sustained negative momentum signals deteriorating market conditions.
■ <−5% ■ −5% to 0% ■ >0%
M2
Yield Curve (10yr − 3mo) ↗
FRED · DGS10 minus DGS3MO · Auto · Daily
Inversion (negative spread) has preceded every US recession since 1970. Re-steepening after inversion often marks the most dangerous phase.
■ <0% ■ 0–1.0% ■ >1.0%
M3
High Yield Credit Spread ↗
FRED · BAMLH0A0HYM2 · Auto · Daily
ICE BofA HY OAS in basis points. Widening spreads signal credit stress and risk-off sentiment. Historically leads equity drawdowns.
■ >500 bps ■ 300–500 bps ■ <300 bps
M4
Investment Grade Credit Spread ↗
FRED · BAMLC0A0CM · Auto · Daily
ICE BofA US Corporate OAS — investment grade (BBB and above). Complements M3 (HY). When IG spreads widen, stress has moved up the credit quality ladder — a more serious signal than HY alone.
■ >1.5% ■ 1.0–1.5% ■ <1.0%
M5
NFCI Financial Conditions ↗
FRED · NFCI · Auto · Weekly (Wed)
Chicago Fed National Financial Conditions Index. Zero = historical average. Positive values = tighter-than-average financial conditions.
■ >0.5 ■ 0–0.5 ■ <0
E1
OECD Composite Leading Indicator ↗
FRED · USALOLITOAASTSAM · Auto · Monthly
Amplitude-adjusted, centered on 100. Above 100 = expansion ahead. Below 100 = slowdown. Lags ~1–2 months. Replaces discontinued Philadelphia Fed USSLIND (2020).
■ <99.0 ■ 99.0–100.5 ■ >100.5
E2
Initial Jobless Claims (4-wk MA) ↗
FRED · IC4WSA · Auto · Weekly (Thu)
4-week moving average smooths weekly volatility. Rising trend above 250K signals labor market deterioration. Above 300K historically associated with recession.
■ >300K ■ 250–300K ■ <250K
E3
Core Capex — Nondefense Capital Goods ex-Aircraft ↗
FRED · NEWORDER · Auto · Monthly · YoY %
The institutional benchmark for business investment intent — strips both defense and aircraft orders. YoY % removes inflation distortion. Declining trend signals businesses pulling back on capex — a leading recession indicator.
■ <−2% YoY ■ −2% to +2% ■ >+2%
E4
SLOOS C&I Lending Standards ↗
FRED · DRTSCILM · Auto · Quarterly
Senior Loan Officer Survey. Net % of banks tightening commercial & industrial lending standards. Tightening credit is a leading indicator of reduced business activity.
■ >20% ■ 0–20% ■ <0%
E5
5-Year Breakeven Inflation ↗
FRED · T5YIEM · Auto · Daily
Market-implied inflation expectations over 5 years (TIPS vs nominal Treasury). Rising expectations above 3% signal inflation risk that could force Fed tightening.
■ >3.0% ■ 2.0–3.0% ■ <2.0%
M6
10-Year Real Yield (TIPS) ↗
FRED · DFII10 · Auto · Daily
The true cost of capital — nominal 10yr yield minus inflation expectations. Rising real yields above 2% historically compress equity P/E multiples and slow investment. A sustained move above 2.5% is a meaningful headwind for risk assets.
■ >2.5% ■ 1.5–2.5% ■ <1.5%
E6
Real Consumer Spending (PCE) ↗
FRED · PCEC96 · Auto · Monthly · YoY %
Real Personal Consumption Expenditures — inflation-adjusted. Covers ~70% of US GDP. YoY % change captures whether the consumer is actually spending. Negative trend confirms economic deterioration already underway.
■ <0% YoY ■ 0–1.5% ■ >1.5%
